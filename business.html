Commercial model & partnerships

RaceVision AI will generate revenue through a tiered subscription model and pay‑per‑race downloads. Professional punters pay a monthly fee for unlimited access to video form guides and advanced analytics, while casual users purchase single race videos on demand. An enterprise tier will offer API access and white‑label feeds for bookmakers, broadcasters and data aggregators.

Partnerships & distribution

Strategic partnerships will accelerate adoption and provide commercial leverage. Collaborating with large global wagering operators gives us direct access to professional punters and marketing reach, while deals with broadcasters and rights holders supply high‑quality video feeds and prominent placement within broadcasts. Trainers and jockeys will act as brand ambassadors, providing authentic endorsements and helping refine analysis through their on‑track experience.

We will offer preferential pricing and co‑marketing opportunities to early B2B partners, ensuring that RaceVision AI becomes the default video‑analysis platform within the racing ecosystem.

Beyond bookmakers, we will engage well‑known trainers and syndicate managers such as the Pike family to champion the product within the racing community. Their endorsements and feedback will help tailor the analysis to practical racing strategies. Retired jockeys will present video breakdowns, lending authority and making the insights more accessible to casual fans. We also envisage integrating the platform into broadcasters’ digital apps, turning RaceVision AI into a second‑screen companion during live telecasts.

Roll‑out and monetisation timeline

Development of the core AI engine and video‑processing infrastructure is expected to take around six months. A revenue‑generating beta release will follow, giving a select group of professionals access to the service. This early stage not only drives initial cash flow but also provides critical feedback for product refinement. A full public launch across key racing territories is slated for 12 months after project initiation, followed by continuous expansion into additional jurisdictions.

Competitive landscape & alternatives

A handful of start‑ups are exploring AI and data services for racing, but none currently offer the automated video‑based form guides we propose. Key comparators include:

  • Alezan.ai: Provides real‑time overlays and safety monitoring for horse racing using computer vision. Focus is on tracking and welfare rather than punter‑focused form analysis.
  • OneSix Technologies: Uses multi‑camera arrays and ultra‑wideband sensors to monitor horses in training and derive physiological metrics. Its business is geared towards injury prevention and coaching, not betting.
  • Traditional form guides: Services such as Timeform and Racing Post compile statistics and written commentary but offer limited video or sectional data. Their format hasn’t evolved to integrate AI‑driven insights.
  • Predictive‑analytics providers: A few machine‑learning services focus on forecasting race outcomes directly, selling tips to punters. RaceVision AI does not pick winners; instead, it empowers users with transparent visual evidence so they can make their own judgements.

Regulatory & ethical considerations

The platform will operate within strict licensing and regulatory frameworks. We will negotiate rights with broadcasters and racing authorities to use video footage legally. In each jurisdiction we will comply with gambling regulations, including any restrictions on the provision of betting data and consumer protections against problem gambling. User accounts will incorporate age verification and responsible‑gaming tools where required.

Data privacy is paramount; our system does not collect personal information about punters, and all analytics are derived from publicly available race videos. We will also adhere to best practices in animal welfare. Insights derived from our analytics may help trainers and regulators identify fatigue or potential injuries, supporting efforts to protect horses and improve safety.

Future expansion & growth opportunities

Once the core platform is established, a number of expansion paths open up:

  • Predictive analytics & betting signals: Using historical performance data and machine‑learning models to generate probability distributions and suggested bets for subscribers.
  • Training & welfare analytics: Offering insights to trainers and veterinarians on stride patterns, recovery and stress indicators to improve performance and animal welfare.
  • Other racing codes: Adapting the technology to greyhound racing, harness racing and even cycling or motor sports, unlocking new markets and revenue streams.
  • Augmented‑reality experiences: Integrating RaceVision AI insights into second‑screen apps and AR glasses so fans at the track or at home can see contextual data overlaid on live footage.
